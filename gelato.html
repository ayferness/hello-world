The Difference Between Gelato and Ice Cream

In the Italian language, gelato is the generic word for ice cream, independent of the style, so every kind of ice cream is referred to as gelato in Italian.In the English, however, the word gelato has come to be used to refer to a specific style of ice cream derived from the Italian artisanal tradition.

Traditional flavors of gelato include custard, vanilla, chocolate, strawberry, hazelnut, pistachio, lemon, raspberry and peach. More modern flavors have developed including pineapple, tiramisu, banana, mango, yogurt, stracciatella, zabajone and amarena and more...

Every other year, international teams compete at the Gelato World Cup (called Coppa del Mondo della Gelateria in Italian). Teams from countries including Japan, Morocco, the United States, Spain, and Poland traveled to Italy to compete in the most recent competition, in January 2016. After the teams made their best gelato dishes, sundaes, and ice sculptures, the Italian team won first place. .
